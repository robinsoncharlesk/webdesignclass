Website Critique Activity

Analyze and Enhance Websites Through Collaboration and Creativity!

Objective

In this activity, you'll work with a partner to critique a website based on Usability, Accessibility, and Aesthetics. You'll provide constructive feedback, engage in meaningful discussions, and add a fun meme or image to express your reactions. This project helps you develop critical thinking, digital citizenship, and collaborative skills.

Instructions

Find a Partner: Pair up with a classmate who shares your interest in web design or a specific website.

Choose a Website: Together, select a website to critique. Here are some ideas:
  • Pop Culture Option: Pick a website from your favorite movie, show, or celebrity.
  • Real-World Challenge: Choose a website from a charity, organization, or local business you care about.
  • Shared Interest: Any website that both of you find interesting!

Explore and Discuss: Spend some time browsing the website with your partner.

Focus Areas: Evaluate the site based on:
  • Usability (1-10): How easy is it to navigate? Can users find what they need without confusion?
  • Accessibility (1-10): Is the site usable for everyone, including people with disabilities? Use tools like WAVE and Chrome's Lighthouse to assess accessibility.
  • Aesthetics (1-10): How appealing is the design? Do the colors, fonts, and layout enhance the user experience?

Express Your Reaction: Spice up your critique by adding a meme or image that represents your thoughts about the website’s design or what you think the design team was aiming for.

Create a Discussion Post: Go to the discussion board and start a new post.

Use the Template Below: Organize your critique with specific examples from the website.

Template: 
Website: [Include the URL here]

Usability: [Write a few sentences about how easy or difficult it was to navigate the site. Mention specific features that helped or hindered usability. Score: 1-10]

Accessibility: [Discuss whether the site is accessible to everyone. Highlight any tools or features that support accessibility or areas where the site could improve. Use the WAVE tool or Lighthouse to check accessibility and mention what you find. Score: 1-10]

Aesthetics: [Describe the overall look and feel of the site. Talk about the design elements like colors, fonts, and images, and how they contribute to the user experience. Score: 1-10]

Fun Enhancement: [Here’s a quirky idea that could improve the site—e.g., add an interactive game, change the color scheme, or make navigation more fun!]

Meme/Image: [Include your meme or image reaction here.]

Read and Respond: Check out at least two other critiques posted by your classmates.

Provide Feedback: Share your thoughts on their evaluations. Do you agree with their assessments? Did they notice something you missed? Use this opportunity to learn from each other!

Grading Criteria

  • Complete Critique: Did you fill out all sections of the template and post your critique on the discussion board?
  • Thoughtful Analysis: Did you and your partner consider all three areas (Usability, Accessibility, Aesthetics) in your critique?
  • Participation: Did you engage with your classmates by responding to at least two other critiques?

Extension Ideas

  • Advanced Accessibility Audit: Use additional tools or techniques to perform a more in-depth accessibility audit of the website.
  • Redesign Proposal: Create a proposal for redesigning one aspect of the website based on your critique.
  • Group Presentation: Present your findings and redesign proposals to the class.

These extensions allow you to dive deeper into web design principles and enhance your analytical and presentation skills. Let me know if you'd like to explore more advanced topics or need additional resources!

Spotify.com: Great Website, But At What Cost

Quick Summary: Spotify is a digital app/website that allows you to listen to music and stream podcasts from around the world. It is currently the best and most popular digital music streaming platform, offering millions of songs and content to choose from. The website is excellent for users who want to stream music and for aspiring artists to share their own music.

Usability (Free): 6/10
Usability (Paid): 9/10
Spotify has many helpful features, such as a sticky playback control bar, high sound quality, easy navigation, and detailed artist information. However, most premium features are locked behind a subscription, making the free version less user-friendly due to excessive advertisements. Reducing ad quantity or finding alternative funding methods could improve usability for free users.

Accessibility: 8/10
Spotify is relatively accessible, with high-contrast text and large fonts. However, issues like empty buttons and missing alt text for images hinder accessibility. Intrusive advertisements also negatively impact the user experience. Overall, while accessibility is good, there are areas for improvement.

Aesthetics: 9/10
Spotify boasts a sleek and minimalist design with a pleasing color scheme of green and black. User-generated content like album covers may clash with the overall theme, but the website remains visually appealing and functional.

Fun Enhancement: To improve the site, Spotify could incorporate more satisfying advertisement experiences for free users, such as contextually relevant ads or interactive ad elements that engage users without disrupting their listening experience.
